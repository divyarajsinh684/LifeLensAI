PRECAUTIONS & GUIDANCE

Evidence-Based
Prevention Protocols

Personalized precautionary guidance tailored to your risk profile — drawn from WHO, ADA, and ACC clinical guidelines.

DIABETES
HEART DISEASE
KIDNEY STONES
GENERAL WELLNESS
🥗
DIET & NUTRITION

Adopt a low-glycemic index diet to moderate postprandial glucose spikes.

  • Limit refined carbohydrates and added sugars
  • Increase dietary fiber from vegetables and legumes
  • Choose whole grains over processed starches
  • Limit sugary beverages — opt for water or unsweetened tea
🏃
PHYSICAL ACTIVITY

Regular aerobic exercise significantly improves insulin sensitivity and glucose control.

  • 150 min/week of moderate aerobic activity (ADA guideline)
  • Resistance training 2–3× per week
  • Avoid prolonged sedentary periods — break up sitting hourly
  • Post-meal walks shown to reduce glucose spikes by 22%
🩺
MONITORING

Regular clinical monitoring enables early detection of progression toward frank diabetes.

  • HbA1c testing every 3–6 months
  • Fasting glucose biweekly if pre-diabetic range
  • Annual comprehensive metabolic panel
  • BMI monitoring and waist circumference tracking
🫀
HEART HEALTH DIET

Heart-protective nutrition reduces LDL cholesterol and systemic inflammation.

  • Mediterranean diet — olive oil, fish, nuts, vegetables
  • Reduce saturated and trans fats consumption
  • Limit dietary sodium to under 2,300 mg/day
  • Increase omega-3 fatty acids from fatty fish
💊
MEDICAL MANAGEMENT

Pharmacological intervention may be warranted based on aggregate risk score.

  • Statin therapy consideration for LDL above 130 mg/dL
  • Blood pressure target below 130/80 mmHg
  • Antiplatelet therapy per cardiologist guidance
  • Annual lipid panel monitoring
🚭
LIFESTYLE FACTORS

Modifiable lifestyle factors account for over 80% of cardiovascular risk reduction potential.

  • Smoking cessation — single highest-impact intervention
  • Limit alcohol to 1–2 standard drinks per day maximum
  • Stress management: meditation, yoga, therapy
  • Prioritize 7–9 hours quality sleep nightly
💧
HYDRATION PROTOCOL

Adequate fluid intake is the single most effective prevention strategy for kidney stones.

  • Target 2.5–3 liters of fluid daily (minimum)
  • Urine should remain pale yellow (diluted)
  • Increase intake in hot weather and post-exercise
  • Citrus water (lemon) raises urinary citrate levels
🥦
DIETARY ADJUSTMENTS

Strategic dietary modifications reduce urinary mineral concentrations that drive stone formation.

  • Limit oxalate-rich foods: spinach, nuts, chocolate, beets
  • Moderate animal protein intake (increases uric acid)
  • Maintain dietary calcium — do NOT restrict it
  • Reduce sodium to prevent hypercalciuria
🔬
URINALYSIS MONITORING

Periodic urine chemistry analysis tracks supersaturation levels before crystal nucleation.

  • 24-hour urine collection every 6 months
  • Monitor urinary pH, calcium, oxalate, uric acid
  • Kidney ultrasound annually if high-risk
  • Report any flank pain or hematuria immediately
😴
SLEEP QUALITY

Sleep deprivation is an independent risk factor for metabolic syndrome, cardiovascular disease, and immune dysfunction.

  • Target 7–9 hours of consolidated sleep nightly
  • Maintain consistent sleep-wake schedule
  • Limit screen exposure 1 hour before bed
  • Screen for sleep apnea if score above 60
🧘
STRESS MANAGEMENT

Chronic cortisol elevation drives glucose dysregulation, inflammation, and cardiovascular strain.

  • Mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR) programs
  • Regular physical activity as primary stress buffer
  • Social connection and community engagement
  • Cognitive behavioral therapy if chronic anxiety
📅
PREVENTIVE SCREENING

Proactive health monitoring enables course correction before pathology becomes entrenched.

  • Annual comprehensive metabolic panel
  • Full LifeLens AI reassessment every 6 months
  • Blood pressure monitoring monthly
  • BMI and waist circumference quarterly
